# KTC Broker Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR)

**Version 1.0 — Effective [DATE TO BE SET BY KTC]**

These Implementing Rules and Regulations ("IRR") govern the accreditation and conduct of customs brokers and their authorized representatives ("Broker", "you") using the KTC Container Terminal Corp. ("KTC") broker portal. By registering for and using the portal, you agree to be bound by this IRR. *[KTC to review with counsel; bracketed items are placeholders.]*

## 1. Definitions

- **Portal** — the KTC online broker job-order system.
- **Broker** — a customs broker or brokerage entity registered and approved on the Portal.
- **Consignee** — the importer/owner of goods on whose behalf services are requested.
- **Job Order** — a request submitted through the Portal for terminal services (e.g. X-ray inspection, DEA/PDEA examination, OOG stripping).
- **KTC** — KTC Container Terminal Corp., the terminal operator.

## 2. Eligibility and Accreditation

- The Broker must be a duly licensed customs broker or an authorized brokerage entity in good standing.
- KTC reserves the right to approve, defer, or reject any registration at its sole discretion.
- Accreditation is non-transferable and may be suspended or revoked for cause (see Section 11).
- KTC may require supporting documents (broker's license, company registration, authorization letters) at registration or thereafter.

## 3. Account Registration and Credentials

- Each Broker account is for the registered Broker only. Credentials must not be shared, sold, or transferred.
- The Broker is responsible for all activity performed under its account.
- The Broker must provide accurate registration details (full name, email, and a valid government-issued ID) and keep them current.
- Suspected compromise of credentials must be reported to KTC immediately.

## 4. Valid Identification and Supporting Documents

- A valid government-issued ID must be uploaded at registration and is subject to KTC verification.
- KTC may require additional identification or authorization documents before approving an account or processing a Job Order.
- Submitting falsified, altered, or fraudulent identification is a serious violation (Section 10).

## 5. Job Order Submission Rules

- All Job Order details — consignee, entry number, container numbers, and requested services — must be accurate, complete, and truthful.
- The Broker is responsible for selecting the correct consignee from the master list and the correct service request for each container.
- Container numbers must correspond to actual containers for which service is sought.
- Job Orders must be submitted in accordance with KTC's operating hours, cut-offs, and procedures *[KTC to specify]*.
- KTC may reject, hold, or require correction of any Job Order containing incomplete, inconsistent, or suspect information.

## 6. Consignee Selection and Accuracy

- The Broker must ensure it is duly authorized to act for the consignee named in a Job Order.
- The Broker warrants that the consignee and shipment information provided is true and correct.
- Misrepresenting the consignee or acting without authority is a violation.

## 7. Fees, Charges, and Payment

- Applicable terminal service fees and charges are as published by KTC *[schedule and payment terms to be specified by KTC]*.
- The Broker is responsible for ensuring fees are settled in accordance with KTC's payment terms.
- Non-payment may result in suspension of service.

## 8. Conduct and Prohibited Acts

The Broker shall not:

- Share, sell, or transfer account credentials, or access the Portal on behalf of an unaccredited party.
- Submit false, misleading, fraudulent, or incomplete information in any Job Order or document.
- Attempt to bypass, probe, or disrupt the Portal's security, access controls, or CAPTCHA.
- Use automated means (bots/scrapers) to access the Portal without written KTC authorization.
- Use the Portal for any unlawful purpose or in violation of customs, PDEA, BOC, or other applicable regulations.
- Interfere with the operations, personnel, or safety procedures of the terminal.

## 9. Data Privacy and Confidentiality

- KTC processes personal and shipment data in accordance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (R.A. 10173) and applicable regulations.
- The Broker must keep confidential any non-public information accessed through the Portal and use it only for legitimate job-order purposes.
- Uploaded documents (e.g. valid IDs, BIR Form 2303) are stored securely and accessed only by authorized KTC personnel.

## 10. Liability and Indemnification

- The Broker is liable for losses, claims, or penalties arising from inaccurate submissions, unauthorized acts, or breach of this IRR.
- The Broker shall indemnify and hold KTC harmless from claims arising out of the Broker's acts or omissions.
- KTC's liability is limited as provided by its terminal service terms and applicable law *[KTC to specify]*.

## 11. Violations, Penalties, Suspension, and Revocation

- Violations of this IRR may result in warning, suspension, or revocation of accreditation, and/or denial of service.
- KTC may immediately suspend an account pending investigation of a suspected serious violation (e.g. fraud, falsified documents, security abuse).
- Penalties and administrative fees, if any, are as determined by KTC *[schedule to be specified]*.
- KTC may report unlawful acts to the appropriate authorities (BOC, PDEA, NBI/PNP, NPC) as warranted.

## 12. Amendments

- KTC may amend this IRR at any time. Material changes will be published on the Portal and the version number updated.
- Continued use of the Portal after an amendment takes effect constitutes acceptance of the revised IRR. KTC may require re-acceptance of a new version.

## 13. Acceptance and Agreement

By ticking the acceptance box at registration, the Broker confirms that it has read, understood, and agrees to be bound by this IRR (the version indicated), and that the information it provides is true and correct.
