03The opportunity

The portal knows the paperwork.
It doesn't yet know the container.

  • Gate logs, yard positions, and repair estimates live in spreadsheets, SharePoint, and memory — invisible to the system and to customers.
  • Every dispute with a shipping line comes down to whose record is believed — and paper loses.
  • Storage days, repair approvals, and handling events are revenue that must be remembered to be billed.
  • Shipping lines increasingly expect electronic gate reports and instant status — the operators who provide them get the volume.
  • The books stay in ERPNext where they belong. What's missing is the operations brain beside them.
  • The systems the industry sells for this are enterprise-priced, decades old, and built for mega-ports. We build the modern one, sized for KTC.

06Phase 2 · The gate

The gate becomes a system,
not a clipboard.

  • Booked arrival windows — trucks stop stacking up; the yard breathes.
  • Digital EIR in minutes — photos, damage marks, seal, weight, signature, at the box.
  • Certified weighing built in — export boxes never bounce for a lapsed scale certificate.
  • Accredited trucker registry — permits tracked with renewal alarms, not binders.
  • Release validation at exit — no box leaves without a matched line reference. Ever.
  • Every gate event lands on the spine — billable, provable, permanent.

09What KTC gains

Control. Capture. Standing.

Revenue capture

Nothing forgotten

Storage days, repairs, handling — billed from records, not recollection. Depot-side charges can run 8–15% of a shipper's logistics cost; capturing them fully is the fastest payback in the plan.

Dispute protection

The record wins

A photo-backed EIR and an unchangeable event history at every handover. When a line disputes a damage or a day count — KTC's record is the strong one.

Market position

The modern operator

Instant status for customers, electronic reporting for lines, KPIs for management. The service level that wins volume — at depot scale, on a modern web stack.

10Why this path is safe

Built the way the portal was built — proven before trusted.

  • It already works this way: the live portal passed 184/184 automated checks, an adversarial break-test, and a blind human walkthrough before launch. Every phase of this plan passes the same production line.
  • Nothing bet on a rewrite: the books stay in ERPNext; the spine grows underneath the running portal; spreadsheets are retired one module at a time — never a big-bang cutover.
  • Independent verification on every money path: no billing logic ships on its author's word alone.
  • Each phase has an exit test — measured in production — before the next begins. A phase that isn't proven doesn't get built on.
