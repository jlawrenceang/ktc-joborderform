## A. TOS market

### Market Overview
- Global TOS market estimates vary widely by research house: ~$521M (2022) → ~$575M (2027) at ~2% CAGR per one analysis; other mills quote $1.2–2.4B (2025/26) rising to $5B by 2035 at 8.7% CAGR — figures are inconsistent/low-confidence, treat as directional only (https://thetius.com/terminal-operating-system-tos-market-overview/, https://marketreportsworld.com/market-reports/terminal-operating-systems-tos-market-14719691)
- Thetius counts ~20 established TOS products from ~17 vendors; segmented 12% startups, 12% scaleups, 47% SMEs, 29% large corporates (https://thetius.com/terminal-operating-system-tos-market-overview/)
- Navis (now Kaleris) is the dominant player: N4 cited as processing 40%+ of global container volume, 350–500+ terminal implementations across 80–92 countries depending on source/date (https://kaleris.com/solutions/terminal-operating-system/container-terminals/, https://thetius.com/terminal-operating-system-tos-market-overview/)
- One market-share estimate (lower-confidence source): Navis/Kaleris ~22-24%, CyberLogitec ~14-16% (strong in Korea/SE Asia/China/Vietnam state ports), top 5 (Navis, CyberLogitec, Inform GmbH, Kaleris, RBS "TOS+") ≈58-62% combined — note Navis and Kaleris are actually one company post-merger, so this stat looks internally confused/AI-generated (https://www.cognitivemarketresearch.com/terminal-operating-system-tos-market-report)
- Cloud-based deployment ~28.8% of market in 2025, fastest-growing segment at ~11.3% CAGR (lower upfront cost, multi-site accessibility, auto-updates) (https://marketintelo.com/report/terminal-operating-system-market)
- Consolidation trend: Accel-KKR bought Kaleris (2020) and Navis from Cargotec for ~$380-415M (2021), then merged them (2022) into one cloud-based logistics-execution platform; Navis had separately absorbed Jade Logistics (Master Terminal, mixed-cargo TOS) (https://www.bglco.com/transactions/kaleris-acquired-navis/, https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/7112-kaleris-and-navis-merge-companies-to-create-cloud-based-logistics-platform, https://thetius.com/terminal-operating-system-tos-market-overview/)

### Open-source TOS / building blocks — verdict: essentially none exist
- "Open-TOS" (github.com/dylreeves/Open-TOS): conceptual only — Django/PostgreSQL planned stack, 2 commits, 0 stars, no working code, just a README; creator cites ~10 yrs terminal IT experience and a gap for African terminals still running manual/paper processes, but the repo delivers nothing usable today

- Net finding: the small-terminal tier is served entirely by commercial SaaS/cloud vendors (Octopi, ContPark, ContVisor, LogProfit, OSCAR, TOPS Expert Cloud, MarineBerth) — no credible, maintained open-source TOS or reusable building-block project was found

### Typical TOS module list (what a vendor sells as "the platform")
- Vessel/berth: berth scheduling/allocation, stowage/vessel planning (e.g., Navis Vessel Autostow), crane allocation/sequencing (https://kaleris.com/solutions/terminal-operating-system/container-terminals/, https://rbs-tops.com/terminal-operating-system/tops-terminal-solution/)
- Yard: slot/stack allocation, yard optimization tied to vessel stowage plan (e.g., CyberLogitec Advanced Yard Allocation, Navis Expert Decking) (https://www.cyberlogitec.com/en/sub/solution/port/opus_terminal.php)
- Gate: truck appointment/booking, RFID/OCR-based access control, in/out processing (https://theintechgroup.com/blog/container-terminal-software-vs-full-tos-guide/)
- Rail: on-dock/near-dock rail planning (dedicated products exist, e.g., Tideworks IPRO) (https://tideworks.com/intermodal-pro/)
- Billing: automated invoicing tied to gate/yard/vessel events, financial-system interfaces (https://rbs-tops.com/terminal-operating-system/tops-terminal-solution/)

- Reefer: monitoring/alerting integration, usually via a third-party RMM system (IDENTEC, Emerson) bridged into the TOS rather than natively built (https://www.rte-usa.com/integrating-terminal-operating-systems-tos-with-remote-reefer-monitoring/)
- M&R (maintenance & repair): equipment/container damage and repair workflow tracking, work-order management — offered as a module even in small-tier SaaS products (ContPark, LogProfit) (https://contpark.com/solutions/container-depot-management-system/)

## B. TOS data models & architecture

### Container Lifecycle States
- Core states: Gate In → Yard (stack) → Load/Discharge (vessel) → Gate Out; TOS also tracks ATA/ATD (actual arrival/departure of vessel) as lifecycle anchors. https://loadmaster.ai/terminal-operating-system-tos/
- Gate-in is a formal receipt event: container physically inspected, and number/type/size/weight/seal/condition are written to the TOS at that moment. https://hz-containers.com/en/glossary/gate-in-container-receipt/
- Reference TOS structure uses 7 modules mapping directly to lifecycle phases: fundamental (reference) data, gate-in, gate-out, discharge, loading, movement management, yard operations. https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/11/6/1648
- TOS acts as single central DB bridging gate/yard/quay so a container's state is consistent across all three subsystems in real time. https://loadmaster.ai/terminal-operating-system-tos/
- **Minimal data model**: `container(id/ISO6346, iso_type_size, status[empty|full], current_state[gate_in|yard|loaded|discharged|gate_out], current_location_fk, owner/operator, last_event_ts)` + append-only `container_event(container_id, event_type, ts, location, actor, source_doc_ref)` — event log is the source of truth; `current_state` is a derived cache.

### Yard Position Model (Block/Bay/Row/Tier)
- Yard divided into Blocks → each block has Bays → each bay has Rows → containers stack in Tiers; position = (block, bay, row, tier). https://contpark.com/yard-location/
- Example real-world code: `IM-A03-07-4` = import area, block A03, row 07, tier 4 — i.e., a composable short-code over the same 4 fields. https://contpark.com/yard-location/
- Every crane/equipment move updates the location code in real time in the TOS/CYMS to prevent double-handling and reduce search time. https://contpark.com/yard-location/
- Vessel-side uses a *different* bay/row/tier system (do not conflate with yard): 6-digit BB-RR-TT; bay numbers run bow→stern, odd=20ft slot/even=40ft slot; rows number outward from centerline (even=one side, odd=other); tiers use even numbers, below-deck low tiers, on-deck starting at 82+. https://www.shippingandfreightresource.com/identifying-a-stow-position/ , https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stowage_plan_for_container_ships
- **Minimal data model**: `yard_slot(id, block, bay, row, tier, max_tier_height, is_reefer_plug, is_hazmat_allowed)` with a unique constraint on (block,bay,row,tier); `container_placement(container_id, slot_id, placed_at, removed_at NULL)` — open row (removed_at IS NULL) = current position; slot occupancy is a query, not a stored flag, to avoid drift.

### Move / Work-Order Queues
- Moves are routed as discrete work orders through the TOS to the dispatcher → crane/equipment operator → yard-truck chain, avoiding duplicate/ambiguous instructions. https://loadmaster.ai/reducing-crane-idle-time-with-better-planning-in-container-terminals/
- Real-time telemetry from cranes/vehicles lets the TOS reroute moves, reassign slots, and flag conflicts dynamically (queue is mutable, not a fixed batch). https://loadmaster.ai/ai-based-workload-balancing-for-wide-span-yard-cranes-in-terminal-operations/
- Straddle/yard equipment receives "next assignment" directly from TOS — i.e., a pull-based single-next-job queue per equipment unit, not a full manifest pushed at shift start. https://loadmaster.ai/throughput-impacts-of-multi-lane-crane-operations-in-automated-port-operations/
- Remote crane operators view the full work queue and can skip/bypass a cell (exception handling built into the queue UI). https://loadmaster.ai/navis-n4-terminal-operating-system/
- **Minimal data model**: `work_order(id, container_id, move_type[discharge|load|yard_shift|gate_relocate], from_slot, to_slot, priority, status[queued|assigned|in_progress|done|exception], assigned_equipment_id, created_at, completed_at)` — one row per physical move; status transitions are the queue engine.

### Gate Processes
- Carriers pre-book arrival slots via an appointment system to smooth traffic and cut congestion; OCR+RFID lanes then auto-identify container/chassis/plate, cutting processing to under ~2 minutes. https://docshipper.com/glossary/in-gate-container-definition-logistics/
- OCR lanes scan container number, seal code, and chassis details as trucks pass; target throughput sub-90 seconds per truck at modern terminals. https://porthouston.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/Bayport_Gate_Process-2017.pdf
- EIR (Equipment Interchange Receipt) is generated at in-gate/out-gate: a legal record of container number, condition, seal integrity, and acceptance timestamp, countersigned/e-acknowledged by both facility and carrier. https://www.freightcourse.com/equipment-interchange-receipt/ , https://www.portcast.io/glossary/eir-equipment-interchange-receipt
- Container numbers follow ISO 6346: 4-letter prefix (3-letter owner code + equipment-category letter) + 6-digit serial + check digit. https://parcelpath.com/eir-in-shipping/
- Damage recorded against a standardized diagram (top/bottom/sides) using damage-type codes (e.g., dent, hole, scratch, structural) — this is the CEDEX code space (ISO 9897), reused later in M&R. https://parcelpath.com/eir-in-shipping/ , https://law.resource.org/pub/in/bis/S13/is.13731.2003.pdf
- **Minimal data model**: `gate_appointment(id, container_id/booking_ref, trucker_id, appointment_window_start/end, lane, status)`; `gate_transaction(id, container_id, direction[in|out], ts, lane, ocr_read_confidence, seal_number, condition_code[], gross_weight, driver_id, chassis_id, eir_doc_id)`; `eir(id, gate_transaction_id, pdf_ref, signed_by_carrier, signed_by_facility, damage_codes[])`.

### Berth Window Planning (Small Terminals)
- Berth allocation weighs draft/LOA (deep water + quay length for larger ships), then priority cargo (perishable/hazmat gets preference). https://www.identecsolutions.com/berth-planning-and-vessel-scheduling-industry-knowledge-hub
- Formal model: static berth allocation ≈ a rectangle-packing problem with vessel release-time constraints, solvable via local search, then re-run inside a real-time/dynamic scheduler as ETAs shift. https://people.orie.cornell.edu/jdai/publications/daiLinMoorthyTeo08.pdf
- Key tunable is the planning time-window length: short window = fast/myopic (misses optimization across vessels), long window = better optimization but degrades as forecast data (ETAs) becomes less reliable further out — directly relevant to a small terminal deciding how far ahead to commit berth slots. https://people.orie.cornell.edu/jdai/publications/daiLinMoorthyTeo08.pdf
- Objective functions are typically minimize vessel waiting time or maximize berth (quay-meter) utilization — small terminals with 1-2 berths effectively reduce to interval-scheduling with these two competing objectives. https://web2-bschool.nus.edu.sg/wp-content/uploads/media_rp/publications/euJxq1431493326.pdf
- **Minimal data model**: `berth(id, quay_length_m, max_draft_m)`; `vessel_call(id, vessel_id, eta, etd, ata NULL, atd NULL, loa_m, draft_m, priority, berth_id NULL, status[requested|confirmed|alongside|departed])` — a single-table interval scheduler is sufficient below ~3 berths; conflicts = overlapping confirmed windows on same berth_id.

### EDI / API Message Set (EDIFACT → DCSA)
- Legacy container terminal EDIFACT set (maintained by SMDG, Shipping Message Development Group): BAPLIE (bay/stowage plan, occupied+empty slots), COPRAR (discharge/load order to terminal), COARRI (discharge/load *report*, actuals back from terminal), CODECO (gate in/out report — confirms delivery/pickup by road/rail/barge carrier, also internal terminal moves), COPARN (booking/empty release), MOVINS (stowage instruction). https://ediacademy.com/blog/codeco-unedifact-message/ , https://smdg.org/documents/edifact-migs-message-implementation-guidelines/container-messages/
- CODECO specifically = "Container Gate-In/Gate-Out Report" confirming interchange with inland carriers (road/rail/barge). https://ediacademy.com/blog/codeco-unedifact-message/
- COARRI = "Container Discharge/Loading Report" — the actuals message showing what was actually loaded/discharged, vs. COPRAR which is the pre-op order. https://ediacademy.com/blog/codeco-unedifact-message/
- DCSA (Digital Container Shipping Association) is positioning itself as the de facto standards body driving EDI → REST/JSON:API replacement; rationale: EDIFACT messages are rigid/over-inclusive (receiver must parse+discard unneeded fields), APIs allow the receiver to request only needed fields and get real-time push instead of batch. https://dcsa.org/newsroom/resources/the-benefits-of-replacing-edi-with-apis-in-container-shipping/
- DCSA Track & Trace standard (current: Interface Standard 2.2, Information Model 3.3, moving to v3.0 beta) defines a common event schema (shipment/transport/equipment events with standardized event names + timestamps) as the API-era successor to COARRI/CODECO-style status reporting; OpenAPI specs published on SwaggerHub / GitHub (dcsaorg/DCSA-TNT, dcsaorg/DCSA-OpenAPI). https://dcsa.org/standards/track-and-trace/standard-documentation-track-and-trace , https://github.com/dcsaorg/DCSA-TNT
- DCSA has also shipped a dedicated API standard for Verified Gross Mass (VGM) exchange as a targeted EDI-message replacement (illustrates the pattern: one API per legacy message family rather than one big API). https://smartmaritimenetwork.com/2025/11/18/dcsa-publishes-api-standard-for-verified-container-weights/
- **Minimal data model**: keep the internal event log container_event (above) and map it to outbound messages via an adapter table `outbound_message(id, event_id_fk, message_type[CODECO|COARRI|BAPLIE|DCSA_TNT_EVENT], format[edifact|json], payload, sent_at, ack_status)` — this decouples core state from whichever wire format a given partner still requires (many small terminals must speak both EDIFACT and DCSA JSON simultaneously through the 2020s transition).

### M&R (Maintenance & Repair) Estimate Flow for Depots
- No standard called "DAMRES" was found; the actual EDIFACT message family for this workflow is: **WESTIM** (owner/lessee's own repair estimate), **DESTIM** (Depot's Equipment Damage & Repair Estimate — depot → owner/lessee), **WORDER** (Work Order/authorization message — owner/lessee authorizes depot to proceed), all layered on **CEDEX** condition/damage codes (ISO 9897) and reported alongside **CODECO** gate events. https://www.1edisource.com/resources/edi-transactions-sets/edi-destim/ , https://www.depotsystems.com/
- Flow: gate-in at depot (CODECO) → damage assessed using CEDEX codes → depot issues DESTIM (estimate: damage description + repair action + cost) → owner/lessee reviews, may forward to lessee → authorization returns as WORDER → depot performs repair against the authorized WORDER. https://ediacademy.com/blog/destim-edifact-transaction/ , https://www.dcs-is-edi.com/edifact/edifact-destim/
- CEDEX = Container Equipment Data Exchange, an internationally standardized alphanumeric code set (component/damage/repair-action codes) enabling depots and owners to exchange precise condition/repair info without free text. https://law.resource.org/pub/in/bis/S13/is.13731.2003.pdf
- **Minimal data model**: `damage_line(id, container_id, gate_transaction_id, cedex_component_code, cedex_damage_code, cedex_repair_code, location_on_container, qty)`; `mr_estimate(id, container_id, damage_line_ids[], depot_id, total_cost, currency, status[draft|sent|approved|rejected], sent_as[DESTIM|WESTIM])`; `work_order_mr(id, estimate_id, authorized_by, authorized_at, status[open|in_repair|complete], wire_message_type='WORDER')`.

### Reefer Monitoring Basics
- Three-part architecture: (1) reefer controller — onboard computer managing setpoint temp + logging trip data, (2) monitoring/telemetry device attached to controller capturing live readings, (3) software platform/dashboard aggregating fleet-wide state + alerts. https://www.rte-usa.com/how-remote-reefer-monitoring-works/
- Parameters tracked beyond temperature: humidity, power supply/genset status, door-open events, and defrost cycles; connectivity via cellular/LTE/5G or site Wi-Fi/LoRa for yard-wide coverage. https://www.identecsolutions.com/news/reefer-container-monitoring-system-it-driven-processes-for-next-level-customer-experience
- Terminal-side value is real-time exception alerting (deviation from setpoint) rather than periodic manual walks, esp. at scale — small terminals may still do scheduled manual genset/temp checks as a fallback tier. https://www.rte-usa.com/how-remote-reefer-monitoring-works/
- Carrier-level platforms (e.g., MSC iReefer) exist as the large-scale reference pattern: continuous per-unit telemetry ingested into a monitoring dashboard with alarms. https://www.msc.com/en/solutions/digital-solutions/ireefer
- **Minimal data model**: `reefer_unit(container_id PK, setpoint_temp, setpoint_humidity, genset_id NULL)`; `reefer_reading(id, container_id, ts, temp_actual, humidity_actual, power_source[grid|genset|none], door_open, alarm_code NULL)` — append-only time series, indexed on (container_id, ts); alerting = a threshold check against setpoint on insert, not a separate stored state.

## C. Depot management systems (the nearest business)

### What Depot Systems Do (distinct from vessel-working terminal TOS)
- Core loop is container-level, not vessel/voyage-level: Gate-In → Yard/Stack placement → Survey/Inspection → M&R (if damaged) → Storage (billed daily) → Gate-Out/Release. (https://containerhub.ai/depot-management-system, https://kaleris.com/who-we-serve/inland-container-depots/)
- Gate ops: every gate-in/out is timestamped and location-tagged and generates a digital EIR (Equipment Interchange Receipt) — the legal proof-of-condition document at handover, with photos, damage codes, container no., weight, seal, signatures. (https://smartgate.tech/blog/understanding-eir, https://hz-containers.com/en/glossary/eir-equipment-interchange-receipt-confirmation-of-equipment-exchange/)
- Yard/stack management: graphical yard maps, yard queues, container lists, location filters, equipment pooling/cycling (incl. twin-lift), aimed at minimizing re-handles and dwell. (https://www.complexica.com/solutions/empty-park-management-system, https://kaleris.com/insights/the-kaleris-dms-an-innovative-solution-to-depot-performance-challenges/)
- Survey types: on-hire (lessor→lessee, sets baseline condition), off-hire (returned to depot, compared to on-hire baseline), and routine gate survey (every in/out); mobile apps now do damage-coding + annotated photos + AI-OCR container-number capture at the box itself. (https://www.container-xchange.com/blog/what-is-a-container-surveyor/, https://amorasurvey.com/services/on-hire-off-hire-condition-survey/, https://conpds.com/digital-documentation-standards-in-modern-container-depots/)
- M&R cycle: certified inspector creates an itemized EOR/estimate (component + damage code + labor norm-hours + material) → sent to owner/lessor/line for approval (approve, reject line-item, request more photo evidence) → repair executed only within approved scope → billed. Pricing keys off a pre-agreed tariff — the line/lessor's own rate card or the IICL standard. Modern platforms let approval auto-route by depot/damage-code/cost-threshold/component so only exceptions need a human. (https://hz-containers.com/en/glossary/mr-estimate-maintenance-repair/, https://hz-containers.com/en/technical-information/estimate-protocol-for-pricing-repair-of-a-shipping-container-from-a-depot/, https://help.container-xchange.com/hc/en-us/articles/5236289467677-What-are-the-Estimate-of-Repairs-Damage-EOR-Classifications)
- Repair standard: IICL "Guide for Container Equipment Inspection," now 6th edition (IICL-6, effective Aug 1 2016, replacing IICL-5), co-published with the International Chamber of Shipping, sets the objective accept/repair criteria used industry-wide for on/off-hire and in-service inspection. (https://www.iicl.org/, https://www.florens.com/pdf/IICL6.pdf)
- Storage billing is depot→line invoicing for yard occupancy — distinct from carrier→customer demurrage/detention — day-counted calendar-wise from gate-in to gate-out against a negotiated free-time + per-diem rate in the depot's service contract; gate/handling fees and M&R markup bill the same way, tied to the movement record. Depot-related charges can run 8-15% of total logistics cost. (https://www.container-xchange.com/blog/port-charges-for-containers, https://docshipper.com/glossary/container-depot-definition-logistics/)
- Release/booking validation: gate-out requires matching the truck/driver against a valid release instruction (equipment release/delivery order) tied to the line's booking or empty-return reference before exit is authorized; condition is re-verified at exit. (https://www.vizionapi.com/blog/container-gate-out-shipping, https://hansatic.com/en/guides/container-booking-process)

### CFS (Container Freight Station) — the adjacent module
- CFS = stuffing/de-stuffing, consolidation/deconsolidation (often bonded) for LCL cargo; unit of work is cargo/SKU inside the box, not the box itself, so it layers customs-hold tracking, cargo manifests, and warehouse put-away on the same gate/EDI backbone as the depot. (https://originsupplychain.com/container-freight-station, https://www.magaya.com/cfs-container-freight-station/)
- CFS vendors overlap heavily with depot vendors (same buyer, adjacent workflow): Origin Supply Chain, ContPark, Omex Infotech, Logi-Sys, GoCargonet — sold as "gate-in to release" automation replacing paper consolidation manifests. (https://originsupplychain.com/insights/software-solution-guide-the-paperless-cfs, https://contpark.com/solutions/container-freight-station-cfs-software/)

### Data Exchange With Shipping Lines: EDI CODECO / Portals / Spreadsheet Reality
- **CODECO** (UN/EDIFACT "Container Gate-In/Gate-Out Report") is the standard message: sent by depot/inland-terminal/CFS, received by shipping agent/forwarder/inland carrier, confirming containers delivered/picked up or reporting internal moves/status changes without physical movement; actively maintained (latest UNECE release D.21A) with an SMDG implementation guide. (https://www.stedi.com/edi/edifact/messages/CODECO, https://service.unece.org/trade/untdid/d21a/trmd/codeco_c.htm, https://smdg.org/wp-content/uploads/MIGs/ContainerMsgs/SMDG-CODECO162.pdf)
- Carriers run their own EDI/portal onboarding programs (e.g., ONE describes itself as "portal neutral," integrated with "almost all major logistics portals," with local portal contacts) — depots must support carrier-specific portals in addition to, or instead of, raw EDI. (https://us.one-line.com/digital-solutions/edi-portal)
- Adoption-gap reality: industry estimates put EDI adoption at only 59-85% of logistics companies despite the tech being decades old, and bad/duplicate/manual data is estimated to cost the average org $15M/yr — a meaningful share of depot-to-line exchange is still manual re-keying, email/PDF, or spreadsheet upload, which is exactly the gap idex24 (iInterchange) and Cofano's multi-format "inbox" are built to absorb. (https://www.shipbob.com/blog/edi-logistics/, https://www.iinterchange.com/, https://www.cofano.nl/en/terminal-operating-system/)
- Depot identity itself needs standardizing before any of this joins up cleanly: BIC Facility Codes (a DCSA-adjacent harmonization effort) are being adopted so each depot has one agreed global ID; DCSA's broader Track & Trace interface standards (latest v2.2) push carriers/depots/third parties toward common API data models instead of one-off EDI/portal pairings. (https://dcsa.org/standards/track-and-trace, https://dcsa.org/newsroom/dcsa-releases-track-trace-interface-standard-version-2-2)

### Modern Web-Native Depot Systems vs. Legacy Incumbents
- Architecture: legacy leaders (e.g., Depot Systems, founded 1998) are licensed/installed client-server software tied to the depot's own infra; newer entrants (Cofano STACK, ContainerHub, Kaleris DMS) are sold explicitly as cloud/SaaS, removing that infra burden. (https://www.cofano.nl/en/terminal-operating-system/, https://www.wisetechglobal.com/news/wisetech-global-acquires-logistics-solutions-company-depot-systems/)
- Capture at the point of work, not after: modern mobile/tablet gate apps do damage-coding + annotated photos + AI-OCR container-number reads + digital signature at the container, generating the EIR instantly and offline-capable for poor-connectivity yards, replacing paper checklists re-keyed later in an office. (https://conpds.com/digital-documentation-standards-in-modern-container-depots/, https://emory.pro/depot-maintenance-container-services/)
- Self-service cuts phone/email load: a client portal lets shipping lines pull their own container status, EIRs, and invoices instead of calling/emailing the depot — a structural change from the fax/phone/email coordination legacy systems assumed. (https://containerhub.ai/depot-management-system)
- Universal-inbox ingestion instead of forcing one format: newer platforms accept EDI, XLSX, PDF, XML, or API and normalize internally, rather than requiring the line to stand up a proper CODECO feed before data can flow. (https://www.cofano.nl/en/terminal-operating-system/, https://www.iinterchange.com/)
- Configurable rules over hard-coded workflow: M&R/billing approval rules configurable by depot/damage-code/cost-threshold/component/customer policy so staff work exceptions only, vs. legacy systems' more rigid one-size workflow. (https://help.container-xchange.com/hc/en-us/articles/5236289467677-What-are-the-Estimate-of-Repairs-Damage-EOR-Classifications)
- AI/agentic layer as newest differentiator: at least one modern entrant (ContainerHub) ships an agentic-AI copilot for depot onboarding/setup and natural-language queries over live depot data — a capability class absent from the legacy-incumbent generation. (https://containerhub.ai/)
- Consolidation pattern: legacy vendors are being rolled up into larger supply-chain-execution platforms (WiseTech/CargoWise Landside absorbing Trinium, Envase, and Depot Systems; Kaleris absorbing Tideworks) rather than rebuilt web-native in place — the incumbents' modernization path is acquisition/bundling, leaving room on UX for standalone web-native challengers. (https://www.cargowise.com/landside/partners/find-a-partner/depot-systems/, https://kaleris.com/who-we-serve/inland-container-depots/)

## D. Philippine regulatory & competitive context

### PPA Regulatory Touchpoints — Private Ports/Depots
- PPA AO 05-2022 (eff. Jul 19, 2022) collapsed private port types to two: commercial and non-commercial; governs development, construction, operation, expansion, rehabilitation, registration and turnover of private ports plus fees/fines. https://www.ppa.com.ph/sites/default/files/issuances_docs/PPA%20A.O.%20NO.%2005-%202022.pdf
- Certificate of Registration (COR) validity is investment-tiered: 5 yrs (₱3–15M small-scale non-commercial), 10 yrs (₱15–50M medium), 25 yrs (₱100M+ large-scale, incl. commercial ports). https://portcalls.com/ppa-overhauls-private-port-operation-policy/
- Applicants must have no pending case/outstanding obligation with PPA; permit processing capped at 20 working days under the Ease of Doing Business Act (RA 11032). https://portcalls.com/ppa-overhauls-private-port-operation-policy/
- Registered private ports must admit PPA officials for inspection at any time and operate per PPA rules/other issuances. https://ppa.com.ph/ppa_services
- PPA AO 04-2016 mandates container weighing (VGM) per SOLAS Ch. VI Reg.2, eff. Jul 1, 2016; export container VGM verified via the terminal/depot's calibrated, NLMP-certified weighbridge; non-compliant boxes are denied loading and incur shutout/storage/re-weigh charges. https://portcalls.com/subic-port-formulates-rules-verified-gross-mass/ ; https://www.scribd.com/presentation/290686003/SOLAS-Container-Weight-Requirement-Ray-Del-Moro-Jr
- PPA AO 01-2022 created a single Transport Accreditation Permit and Pass for Ports (TAPP) for all port-servicing truckers/haulers — 3-year validity, renewal filing due 60 days pre-expiry, requires corporate docs, ITR, truck OR/CR list. https://www.ppa.com.ph/content/truckers-laud-ppa-new-permitting-system
- All "port service providers" (hauling/forwarding/cargo-handling/CY-CFS-adjacent services) require separate PPA accreditation on top of BOC accreditation. https://www.ppa.com.ph/sites/default/files/issuances_docs/Guidelines%20on%20the%20Accreditation%20of%20Port%20Service%20Providers.pdf
- Live jurisdictional fight: PPA AO 04-2021 (Trusted Operator Program–Container Registry & Monitoring System, TOP-CRMS) plus its Empty Container Storage Shared Service Facility (ECSSF) would require empty-depot operators to register/monitor every container digitally; ECSSF enrollment requires the private CY to already be BOC-authorized. https://www.maritimegateway.com/guidelines-released-to-formally-operate-empty-container-depots/
- 10+ named CY/depot operators (Sea Container Depot Corp., TBS Container Depot, Inland Container Depot, Brightpoint Logistics, others) plus ACYOP and ACOP industry associations opposed TOP-CRMS, citing ~₱35B/yr added import cost (up to ~50% cost increase claims). https://mb.com.ph/2023/01/12/trade-groups-want-ppas-container-registration-order-scrapped/ ; https://rappler.com/business/top-crms-paused-opposition-fears-higher-shipping-costs/
- ARTA formally found "no sound legal and empirical basis" for TOP-CRMS; PPA Board suspended it indefinitely from Jan 2023, but PPA has separately been reported proceeding with a ₱877.6M container-monitoring project — status remains unsettled/contested, not repealed. https://portcalls.com/no-legal-basis-for-ppa-container-monitoring-system-says-arta/ ; https://www.portcalls.com/ppa-proceeds-with-p877-6m-container-monitoring-project/
- CMTA-based turf dispute: PPA has no legal authority to accredit off-dock/off-terminal facilities per RA 10863 — that's BOC's function — yet PPA's private-port and container-staging/SSF orders keep reaching into off-dock depot space. https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/10/40599 (see also Sunstar: "Container yard operators: PPA intends to take over business")

### BOC Integration Points
- Legal basis: Customs Modernization and Tariff Act (RA 10863, CMTA) Title VIII Ch.2 governs Customs Facilities and Warehouses (CFWs) — CY, CFS, seaport/airport temp storage, off-dock CFW, off-terminal CFW, inland container depots. https://customs.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/cmo-18-2022-Rules-and-Regulations-on-the-Establishment-Supervision-Operation-and-Control-of-CFWs.pdf
- CAO 09-2019 + CMO 18-2022 implement CFW establishment/supervision/operation/control rules; existing PPA-contracted terminal operators can be authorized as CFW subject to added BOC conditions; all existing "public bonded warehouses" were reclassified as CFWs. https://portcalls.com/boc-rules-on-setting-up-cfws-now-in-effect/
- CAO 08-2019 (Sept 25, 2019) is BOC's first comprehensive container accounting/monitoring regime for loaded and empty boxes at seaports, aligned to the 1972 Customs Convention on Containers and Revised Kyoto Convention. https://customs.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/CAO-08-2019-Policies_on_Admission_Movement_and_Re-exportation_of_Containers_at_the_Seaports_up1.pdf
- All CY/CFS are legally "customs zone" / Authorized Customs Facility (ACF), open to BOC officials on demand for ocular inspection at any time. https://customs.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/cmo-30-2015-rules-regulations-for-the-establishment-supervision-control-of-wharves-cy-cfs-warehouses-examination-areas-other-facilities-wn-the-customs-zone-otherwise-.pdf
- E-TRACC (Electronic Tracking of Containerized Cargo, CMO 04-2020): GPS-enabled electronic customs seals (ECS) mandatory for any transfer of cargo to a CY/CFS or other customs facility, transit to free zones/depots/terminals/bonded warehouses, and shipments flagged for further verification; expanded to barge/domestic-vessel inland+sea transfers via CMO 09-2024; rolled out to BOC Davao specifically. https://customs.gov.ph/boc-further-intensifies-border-protection-efforts-with-e-tracc/ ; https://portcalls.com/boc-davao-implements-e-tracc/
- X-ray Inspection Project (XIP): BOC operates fixed + mobile container X-ray machines as an alternative to physical exam (legal basis CAO 001-02); BOC is moving X-ray images to a centralized cloud platform to prevent tampering. https://customs.gov.ph/boc-x-ray-inspection-project-yields-success-in-safeguarding-national-borders/ ; https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/2184819/customs-reforms-include-tapping-ai-cloud-tech
- National Single Window (NSW, EO 482) + ASEAN Single Window (live since Dec 30, 2019) centralize e-permits/clearances; CMO 12-2012-style e-permit tagging is meant to block fake/recycled import-export permits — a depot's downstream system needs to reconcile against NSW-issued authorizations. https://www.nsw.gov.ph/ ; https://portcalls.com/ph-customs-rule-authenticates-e-permits-under-national-single-window/
- BOC drafted a CAO regulating shipping-line charges: bans container-deposit liens tied to unrelated past transactions, bans withholding deposit refunds >15 days over unrelated D&D collectibles, bars detention charges when the delay is carrier-caused (e.g., no yard space for empty return) — directly shapes a depot's empty-return SLAs with lines. https://customs.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/CAO-on-Regulation-of-Charges-Imposed-by-Shipping-Lines_v4.pdf

### Shipping-Line Depot Requirements / EDI
- Standard container-movement EDI is UN/EDIFACT: COPARN (pre-notify/booking — release-empty-for-packing, accept-container-at-facility instructions; sender/receiver roles include container depot, CFS, terminal, lessor) and CODECO (gate-in/gate-out confirmation report from depot/terminal back to carrier/agent). https://smdg.org/wp-content/uploads/MIGs/ContainerMsgs/SMDG-COPARN162.pdf ; https://ediacademy.com/blog/codeco-unedifact-message/
- In practice PH carriers still run high-touch, non-EDI processes alongside EDI: CMA CGM requires manual email pre-advise + slot confirmation (1 BL, max 10 containers/request) to nominated terminal/CY mailboxes before empty return, checked against Last Free Time on the Delivery Order. https://www.cma-cgm.com/local/philippines/news/210/philippines-update-on-pre-advise-procedure-for-empty-equipment-delivery-in-manila-and-batangas
- Carriers designate/rotate which depot(s) a consignee must return an empty to, and are barred from charging detention when their own yard-space shortage causes the delay — a compliance constraint depots' capacity-signaling must respect. https://portcalls.com/container-yards-return-empties-cmacgm-apl-hyundai-ts-lines-ph-clients/
- Maersk's inbound free-time clock (Day 1) starts the same day of discharge and runs until empty return at the depot, across multiple depot options per client convenience — timestamp accuracy at gate-in is directly tied to carrier billing. https://www.maersk.com/local-information/asia-pacific/philippines/import
- Industry pattern flagged broadly: shipping lines/terminals still reconcile fragmented, differently-formatted systems manually, causing data-entry errors and delayed/missed D&D billing capture — the generic failure mode a depot's TOS integration must solve per carrier. https://www.solverminds.com/2025/04/18/container-management-how-leading-shipping-lines-are-turning-operational-challenges-into-strategic-advantage/

### Local Competitive Landscape (Davao/Mindanao)
- Davao International Container Terminal (DICT), Panabo — ANFLOCOR/Dole-Stanfilco JV — is the only Mindanao port handling Panamax–Post-Panamax vessels; runs an Empty Container Depot (ECD) ~400m from the port (pre-trip inspection, wash, M&R, reefer setting, releasing). https://www.dict.com.ph/ ; https://lca.logcluster.org/21323-philippines-mindanao-davao-international-container-port
- Davao Port Complex (DICT/ICTSI-operated terminal) handled 1.85M TEU in 2024, nearing its ~2.1M TEU ceiling; a $45M Phase 2 expansion (targeted end-2026) adds 1.2M TEU/yr capacity + 150 reefer plugs. https://kitalent.com/articles/article-davao-port-logistics-talent-gap/
- ICTSI separately operates Mindanao Container Terminal at Phividec Industrial Estate, Tagoloan, Misamis Oriental (24 ha, containerized + non-containerized cargo) — the CDO-area alternative to Davao. https://www.ictsi.com/our-offering/our-terminals/mindanao-container-terminal
- Government-owned Davao-Sasa Port's PPP modernization (BTO landlord-port concession, 30-yr term) has stalled for over a decade — bidding postponed 3x, ICTSI's bidding interest cooled, city council non-consultation issues; DICT's owner Anflocor publicly demands a "level playing field," opposing any mandate to route cargo to Sasa. https://portcalls.com/ictsis-interest-in-davao-sasa-port-upgrade-cools/ ; https://www.portcalls.com/davao-international-container-operator-calls-levelplaying-field-davaosasa/
- Other private cargo-handling players Anflocor names as already meeting Davao-area container needs: Terminal Facilities and Services Corp., Sumifru, Unifrutti, Dole/Pacinter, plus planned Hijo International Port Services — a fragmented, agri-export-driven private-terminal landscape. https://www.portcalls.com/davao-international-container-operator-calls-levelplaying-field-davaosasa/
- Sasa's prior state-linked cargo-handler, DIPSSCOR, ceased operations Jun 30 2022. https://www.facebook.com/TheManilaTimes/posts/international-container-terminal-services-inc-ictsis-subsidiary-davao-integrated/10159506290572012/
- Davao port congestion (vessel-call backlog) hit an all-time high of 111 units in Apr 2025 vs a 42-unit 2022–2025 average — a capacity/throughput signal for any depot sizing decisions. https://www.ceicdata.com/en/philippines/port-congestion-calls-by-port-and-vessel-type
- Structural constraint, not just capital: TOS implementation, controlled-atmosphere reefer management, and China pre-clearance for perishables are specialist skills with "dozens, not hundreds" of qualified people in all of Mindanao — talent, not tech spend, is the binding constraint on scaling ops. https://kitalent.com/articles/article-davao-port-logistics-talent-gap/

### PEZA / Economic-Zone Angles
- Anflo Industrial Estate (AIE), Panabo — 63 ha, 24 operating locators (agro-industry, manufacturing/packaging, logistics) — sits ~300m from DICT; same ANFLOCOR group links the ecozone and the container terminal. https://anfloindustrialestate.com/
- Metro Davao Economic Zone (MDEZ), Digos City, is a second flagship southern-Mindanao ecozone (industrial/agro-industrial/IT-BPM). https://www.peza.gov.ph/press-releases/peza-ecozones-davao-serve-gateway-world
- Mindanao-wide: 42 operating PEZA ecozones hosting 107 registered enterprises as of Mar 2025; PEZA and Mindanao Development Authority (MinDA) signed an MOU aligning ecozone expansion to the Mindanao Development Corridors Framework. https://minda.gov.ph/news/380-minda-peza-boosts-ties-for-mindanao-ecozones-devt
- PEZA "Ecozone Logistics Service Enterprise" (ELSE) registration lets a warehouse/logistics operator serve ecozone locators' import/export, inventory and JIT needs, with VAT zero-rating recognized as an eligible activity — a possible incentive path if a depot co-locates with/serves PEZA-registered exporters. https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=1aea564c-634a-423e-b191-2d79a7d8698e
- BOC issued rules for electronic (e-transfer) processing of goods moving from an ecozone logistics facility to its partner locator — meaning a depot integrated with a PEZA-zone client needs a second, PEZA-specific electronic-transfer compliance track layered on top of standard BOC CY-CFS processing. https://www.portcalls.com/customs-sets-rules-eprocessing-goods-transfer-ecozone-logistics-facility-locator/

### Compliance/Integration Walls Likely Hit in Years 2–5
- Dual/competing accreditation stacks stacking up as volume grows: BOC CFW/CY-CFS accreditation (CMTA/CAO 09-2019/CMO 18-2022) + PPA private-port or port-service-provider accreditation + (if unfrozen) PPA's TOP-CRMS/ECSSF container-registry mandate — three overlapping, historically contested regimes with no settled jurisdictional line (PPA vs BOC dispute over off-dock facilities is still legally live). https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/10/40599 ; https://www.maritimegateway.com/guidelines-released-to-formally-operate-empty-container-depots/
- Per-carrier EDI fragmentation: formal EDIFACT COPARN/CODECO support expected by global lines, but Philippine-market reality mixes that with manual email pre-advise workflows (e.g., CMA CGM's 10-container/email cap) — a depot scaling past a handful of lines needs to support both a standards-based integration and multiple bespoke manual choke points simultaneously. https://smdg.org/wp-content/uploads/MIGs/ContainerMsgs/SMDG-COPARN162.pdf ; https://www.cma-cgm.com/local/philippines/news/210/philippines-update-on-pre-advise-procedure-for-empty-equipment-delivery-in-manila-and-batangas
- E-TRACC GPS-seal compliance becomes a per-movement integration dependency (not one-time): every inland transfer to/from the depot needs an electronic customs seal event logged, now extended to barge/sea legs too (CMO 09-2024) — depot dispatch/TMS must talk to BOC's tracking system, not just the carrier's. https://customs.gov.ph/boc-further-intensifies-border-protection-efforts-with-e-tracc/
- VGM weighbridge compliance is a hard gate, not a soft one: uncalibrated/uncertified weighing (NLMP certification lapses) blocks export loading outright and triggers shutout/storage/stripping charges — a scaling depot adding weighbridge capacity must keep pace with certification renewal cycles. https://www.scribd.com/presentation/290686003/SOLAS-Container-Weight-Requirement-Ray-Del-Moro-Jr
- Regulatory-cost volatility on the commercial side: BOC's draft CAO on shipping-line charges (container deposit/D&D rules) and PPA's storage-tariff regime both directly move the depot's revenue model (storage fees, deposit float, D&D pass-through) and are still in flux/contested. https://customs.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/CAO-on-Regulation-of-Charges-Imposed-by-Shipping-Lines_v4.pdf
- Capacity/talent ceiling ahead of tech: Davao's terminal is nearing its throughput ceiling pre-expansion, and the regional labor pool for TOS operation and specialized cargo handling is described as "dozens, not hundreds" — a depot's growth plan collides with a labor-supply wall before it collides with a systems-capacity wall. https://kitalent.com/articles/article-davao-port-logistics-talent-gap/
- PEZA-adjacency adds a second electronic-transfer compliance surface (ecozone-locator e-transfer rules) on top of standard BOC CY-CFS processing for any depot serving ecozone-registered exporters (a likely Davao-area client base given AIE/MDEZ). https://www.portcalls.com/customs-sets-rules-eprocessing-goods-transfer-ecozone-logistics-facility-locator/
- Trucking-network accreditation churn: PPA's TAPP (3-yr cycle, 60-day pre-expiry renewal filing) sits on top of any BOC/LTFRB hauler requirements — a growing depot's outsourced trucking base multiplies renewal-tracking overhead linearly with fleet size. https://www.ppa.com.ph/content/truckers-laud-ppa-new-permitting-system
